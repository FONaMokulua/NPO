Aloha, Welcome to Friends of Nā Mokulua

We are a wildlife and nature conservation organization dedicated to the preservation of the Mokulua Islands. Our mission is to protect the islands' unique ecosystem while ensuring safety and sustainability for those visiting the area.

About Us

Our Story: The Smith Family founded Friends of Nā Mokulua with the core mission of preserving the Mokulua Islands for future generations. As residents of the area, we've always had a deep love for these islands—spending countless hours exploring their beauty and enjoying everything they have to offer. The Mokulua Islands are not just a special place to us; they hold significant cultural, ecological, and spiritual value. We wanted to create an opportunity for others to experience these same wonders while ensuring their protection. However, over time, we began to witness the growing impact of tourism. People visiting the islands, some unfamiliar with its fragile ecosystem, were unintentionally harming the environment. We also saw visitors getting hurt due to the challenging and rocky landings on the islands. It became clear that something needed to be done to ensure the safety of both the visitors and the environment.

Mission: Our mission is simple: Malama ʻAina—care for the land. We founded Friends of Nā Mokulua to help preserve and protect the Mokulua Islands' natural beauty and unique ecosystems for future generations. We aim to educate visitors about the dangers of the island, the rules for preserving its natural beauty, and to assist those unfamiliar with the area in staying safe while respecting the ecosystem. We offer services such as helping tourists safely dock their boats, guiding them through the island’s terrain, picking up trash, and ensuring that both people and the environment are protected.

Vision: Our vision is to maintain the beauty and safety of the Mokulua Islands while offering help to tourists and visitors. We want to continue preserving the islands' unique flora and fauna, creating a sustainable balance where both the environment and visitors can coexist in harmony. We believe in long-term preservation, ensuring that the islands remain a pristine and safe place for future generations to enjoy. We aim to raise awareness of the islands' ecological significance and inspire responsible stewardship of this special area. Through education and direct action, we can make sure the islands’ delicate balance is maintained for years to come.

Our Contribution: As a family deeply connected to the Mokulua Islands, we’ve been actively involved in the preservation of these islands. We've paddled out to the Mokulua Islands countless times, cleaning up trash when we're there, helping visitors safely land, supporting them in navigating the challenging terrain, and educating them on the rules of the land, kindly. It’s always been important to us to ensure that the islands remain a safe and beautiful destination for everyone. We’ve also worked to raise awareness about the importance of maintaining the islands' natural state and educating others on how they can help protect this precious environment. By offering hands-on support and providing information, we hope to inspire others to join us in preserving the Mokes for future generations to enjoy.

Our Services

Assistance for Tourists

Navigating to the Mokulua Islands can be challenging, especially for first-time visitors, due to the rocky shores and unpredictable waters. Our team provides essential assistance with safely crossing the water, docking, and undocking boats. We guide visitors through the process, ensuring both water safety and proper boat handling, while also educating them on respecting the island's wildlife and ecosystem. Our goal is to ensure a safe, enjoyable experience for everyone, protecting both the visitors and the Mokulua Islands' fragile environment.

Wildlife Protection

The Mokulua Islands are home to several endangered species, including native seabirds such as the Laysan albatross and Hawaiian monk seals. These species rely on the islands for nesting, breeding, and hauling out. To protect these vulnerable animals, we take great care in educating visitors about how to respect the island’s wildlife and ecosystem. Our team enforces island rules, such as maintaining safe distances from nesting areas and marine life, ensuring the safety of both visitors and animals. By monitoring sensitive habitats, we help prevent human disturbance and protect the natural balance of the island, allowing future generations to experience its beauty.

Environmental Stewardship

We are deeply committed to preserving the Mokulua Islands by minimizing human impact and keeping the islands free from litter. We try our best to ensure that the islands’ natural beauty remains intact for future generations. We encourage visitors to actively participate in these initiatives, as we believe that collective action is vital to maintaining a clean and healthy environment. Additionally, we educate tourists on the importance of leaving no trace, emphasizing the need to pack out everything they bring to the island. By reducing waste and promoting sustainable practices, we help safeguard the islands' delicate ecosystem and prevent long-term environmental damage.

Guidance and Education

Our team helps educate others on the importance of knowing your limits and surroundings when on the Mokes and the beach, and also helps them gain a deeper understanding of the Mokulua Islands' cultural significance, rich history, and the vital need for their preservation. Visitors learn about the unique flora and fauna of the islands, including endangered species like native seabirds and Hawaiian monk seals. We also emphasize the history of the Hawaiian Islands and their cultural importance, providing insights into how the Mokes have been used by Hawaiians for centuries. Through this educational experience, we inspire visitors to become responsible travelers, fostering a deeper connection to the environment. Our goal is to encourage everyone to actively participate in the conservation of the Mokulua Islands and similar natural reserves across Hawaii, ensuring these beautiful places are preserved for future generations.

About the Mokulua Islands

The Mokulua Islands, or "Mokes," as locals affectionately call them, are two small yet incredibly scenic islands located off the windward coast of Oahu, Hawaii. Known for their stunning landscapes, unique wildlife, and deep cultural history, the islands are a true gem of the Hawaiian archipelago. The Mokes are home to several endangered species, including native seabirds like the Laysan albatross and Hawaiian monk seals that haul out on the beaches and use the area for pupping.

As beautiful as these islands are, they also require special care and attention. Access is carefully regulated to protect the fragile ecosystem, and visitors must follow specific rules to ensure they don’t harm the environment.

Mokulua Islands Visitor Guidelines:

  • No Littering: Please pack out everything you bring to the islands. Keep the environment clean and beautiful for everyone to enjoy.
  • Wildlife Respect: Stay a safe distance from all wildlife, especially the nesting seabirds and Hawaiian monk seals. Do not disturb or feed the animals.
  • No Camping: Camping is prohibited on the islands to minimize human impact and preserve the natural habitats.
  • Stay on Designated Paths: Stick to established trails and avoid stepping on sensitive vegetation or wildlife nesting areas.
  • No Fires: For safety and environmental reasons, fires are not permitted on the islands.
  • Respect Cultural Sites: The Mokulua Islands hold cultural significance, so please respect all historical sites and avoid disturbing sacred areas.

Whether you're a first-time visitor or someone who's come to appreciate the beauty of the Mokes, it’s crucial to respect the land and its wildlife.

Historically, the Mokes were used by ancient Hawaiians as a place of refuge and for fishing, and their cultural importance remains strong today. Here at Friends of Nā Mokulua, we're passionate about preserving the islands' natural beauty, educating visitors about their ecological significance, and ensuring that the Mokes remain a sanctuary for both wildlife and people alike. Our work is all about keeping this incredible place safe, clean, and accessible for future generations, so that everyone can enjoy the wonders of the Mokulua Islands as much as we do.
